# IRS Forms Reference Guide

This document explains which IRS forms are used and how the Freedom US Tax Return application maps to them.

## Main Form

### Form 1040 - U.S. Individual Income Tax Return
**Always required** - This is the main tax return form for all individual taxpayers.

**Application Mapping:**
- Lines 1-9: Income section → Income page
- Lines 10-11: Adjustments section → Income page (adjustments)
- Line 12: Standard/Itemized deductions → Deductions page
- Line 15: Taxable income (calculated)
- Line 16: Tax (calculated)
- Lines 17-24: Credits → Credits page
- Lines 25-33: Payments → Payments page
- Lines 34-37: Refund or amount owed (calculated)

**Personal Information:**
- Name, SSN, address → Personal Information page
- Filing status → Filing Status page
- Dependents → Personal Information page

## Schedules to Form 1040

### Schedule 1 - Additional Income and Adjustments to Income
**Required when:** You have additional income or adjustments not reported on main Form 1040

**Part I - Additional Income:**
- Line 1: Taxable refunds of state/local taxes
- Line 2: Alimony received
- Line 3: Business income (from Schedule C)
- Line 4: Other gains or losses
- Line 5: Rental real estate (from Schedule E)
- Line 6: Farm income
- Line 7: Unemployment compensation
- Line 8: Other income

**Part II - Adjustments to Income:**
- Line 10: Educator expenses
- Line 11: Business expenses (unreimbursed)
- Line 12: Health savings account deduction
- Line 13: Moving expenses (military)
- Line 14: Self-employment tax deduction
- Line 15: Self-employed retirement plans
- Line 16: Self-employed health insurance
- Line 17: Penalty on early withdrawal of savings
- Line 18: Alimony paid
- Line 19: IRA deduction
- Line 20: Student loan interest deduction
- Line 21: Tuition and fees
- Line 22: Other adjustments

**Application Mapping:** Income page (Other Income section) and future Adjustments page

### Schedule 2 - Additional Taxes
**Required when:** You owe additional taxes beyond regular income tax

**Part I - Tax:**
- Line 1: Alternative minimum tax
- Line 2: Excess advance premium tax credit repayment
- Line 3: Self-employment tax (from Schedule SE)
- Line 4: Social security and Medicare tax (unreported)
- Line 5: Additional Medicare tax
- Line 6: Net investment income tax
- Line 7: Other taxes

**Part II - Other Taxes:**
- Line 8: Additional taxes from forms 8959, 8960, etc.

**Application Mapping:** Credits page (Other Taxes section)

### Schedule 3 - Additional Credits and Payments
**Required when:** You have tax credits or payments not on main Form 1040

**Part I - Nonrefundable Credits:**
- Line 1: Foreign tax credit
- Line 2: Child and dependent care expenses credit
- Line 3: Education credits
- Line 4: Retirement savings contributions credit
- Line 5: Residential energy credits
- Line 6-7: Other credits

**Part II - Other Payments and Refundable Credits:**
- Line 8: Net premium tax credit
- Line 9: Amount paid with extension
- Line 10: Excess social security tax withheld
- Line 11: Credit for federal tax on fuels
- Line 12-14: Other payments

**Application Mapping:** Credits page

### Schedule A - Itemized Deductions
**Required when:** You choose to itemize deductions instead of taking standard deduction

**Medical and Dental Expenses:**
- Line 1: Medical and dental expenses
- Line 2: AGI × 7.5%
- Line 3: Deductible amount (Line 1 - Line 2)

**Taxes You Paid:**
- Line 5: State and local taxes (max $10,000)
  - Income taxes OR sales taxes
  - Real estate taxes
  - Personal property taxes

**Interest You Paid:**
- Line 8: Home mortgage interest
- Line 9: Investment interest

**Gifts to Charity:**
- Line 11: Cash contributions
- Line 12: Non-cash contributions

**Casualty and Theft Losses:**
- Line 15: Casualty and theft losses (from Form 4684)

**Other Itemized Deductions:**
- Line 16: Other deductions

**Application Mapping:** Deductions page (Itemized Deductions section)

### Schedule B - Interest and Ordinary Dividends
**Required when:** Interest or dividend income exceeds $1,500

**Part I - Interest:**
- List all payers of interest income
- Total interest income

**Part II - Ordinary Dividends:**
- List all payers of dividends
- Total ordinary dividends

**Part III - Foreign Accounts and Trusts:**
- Questions about foreign financial accounts

**Application Mapping:** Income page (Interest and Dividend sections)

### Schedule C - Profit or Loss from Business
**Required when:** You have self-employment income from a sole proprietorship

**Sections:**
- Part I: Income (gross receipts, returns, cost of goods sold)
- Part II: Expenses (detailed business expenses)
- Part III: Cost of goods sold
- Part IV: Information on vehicle
- Part V: Other expenses

**Calculates:** Net profit or loss from business

**Application Mapping:** Future Self-Employment page

### Schedule D - Capital Gains and Losses
**Required when:** You sold stocks, bonds, real estate, or other capital assets

**Part I - Short-Term Capital Gains:**
- Assets held 1 year or less

**Part II - Long-Term Capital Gains:**
- Assets held more than 1 year

**Part III - Summary:**
- Net capital gain or loss

**Application Mapping:** Future Investments page

### Schedule E - Supplemental Income and Loss
**Required when:** You have rental income, royalties, partnerships, S corporations, estates, or trusts

**Part I - Rental Income:**
- Rental income and expenses
- Net rental income

**Parts II-V:**
- Partnership/S corporation income
- Estates and trusts
- Real estate mortgage investment conduits

**Application Mapping:** Future Rental Income page

### Schedule EIC - Earned Income Credit
**Required when:** You claim the Earned Income Credit with qualifying children

**Information Required:**
- Child's name
- Child's SSN
- Child's year of birth
- Relationship to you
- Months child lived with you
- Whether child is a student or disabled

**Application Mapping:** Credits page (EIC section)

### Schedule SE - Self-Employment Tax
**Required when:** You have net earnings from self-employment of $400 or more

**Sections:**
- Part I: Self-Employment Tax (short form)
- Part II: Optional methods

**Calculates:**
- Self-employment tax (Social Security and Medicare)
- Deduction for one-half of SE tax

**Application Mapping:** Calculated from Schedule C data

### Schedule 8812 - Credits for Qualifying Children and Other Dependents
**Required when:** You claim the Child Tax Credit or Additional Child Tax Credit

**Information:**
- Number of qualifying children
- Child Tax Credit calculation
- Additional Child Tax Credit (refundable portion)

**Application Mapping:** Credits page (Child Tax Credit section)

## Common Additional Forms

### Form W-2 - Wage and Tax Statement
**Source:** Provided by employers

**Key Information:**
- Box 1: Wages, tips, other compensation
- Box 2: Federal income tax withheld
- Box 3: Social security wages
- Box 4: Social security tax withheld
- Box 5: Medicare wages and tips
- Box 6: Medicare tax withheld

**Application Mapping:** Income page (W-2 section)

### Form 1099-INT - Interest Income
**Source:** Banks, credit unions, other financial institutions

**Key Information:**
- Box 1: Interest income
- Box 3: Interest on U.S. Savings Bonds
- Box 8: Tax-exempt interest

**Application Mapping:** Income page (Interest section)

### Form 1099-DIV - Dividends and Distributions
**Source:** Investment companies, corporations

**Key Information:**
- Box 1a: Total ordinary dividends
- Box 1b: Qualified dividends
- Box 2a: Total capital gain distributions

**Application Mapping:** Income page (Dividend section)

### Form 1099-MISC - Miscellaneous Income
**Source:** Various payers

**Key Information:**
- Box 1: Rents
- Box 2: Royalties
- Box 3: Other income
- Box 8: Substitute payments

**Application Mapping:** Future Other Income section

### Form 1099-NEC - Nonemployee Compensation
**Source:** Businesses that paid you for services

**Key Information:**
- Box 1: Nonemployee compensation (for independent contractors)

**Application Mapping:** Future Self-Employment section

### Form 1099-R - Distributions from Retirement Plans
**Source:** Retirement plan administrators

**Key Information:**
- Box 1: Gross distribution
- Box 2a: Taxable amount
- Box 7: Distribution code

**Application Mapping:** Future Retirement Income section

### Form 1099-G - Government Payments
**Source:** Government agencies

**Key Information:**
- Box 1: Unemployment compensation
- Box 2: State/local tax refunds
- Box 6: Taxable grants

**Application Mapping:** Income page (Unemployment section)

### Form 8863 - Education Credits
**Required when:** You claim education credits (AOTC or LLC)

**Information:**
- Student information
- Qualified expenses
- Adjusted qualified expenses
- Credit calculation

**Application Mapping:** Credits page (Education Credits section)

## Form Filing Requirements

### When You Must File
You generally must file if:
- **Single:** Income over $15,750 (2025)
- **Married Filing Jointly:** Income over $31,500 (2025)
- **Head of Household:** Income over $23,625 (2025)

### Special Filing Requirements
Even if below thresholds, file if:
- You had self-employment income of $400 or more
- You owe special taxes (alternative minimum tax, etc.)
- You want to claim a refundable credit
- You received HSA, MSA, or Archer MSA distributions

## Application Form Determination Logic

The application automatically determines which forms you need based on:

1. **Personal Information** → Always requires Form 1040
2. **Income Types:**
   - W-2 income → Form 1040 (no additional schedule)
   - Interest > $1,500 → Schedule B
   - Dividends > $1,500 → Schedule B
   - Self-employment income → Schedule C, Schedule SE
   - Capital gains/losses → Schedule D
   - Rental income → Schedule E
   - Unemployment → Schedule 1

3. **Deductions:**
   - Itemized deductions → Schedule A
   - Standard deduction → Form 1040 only

4. **Credits:**
   - Child Tax Credit → Schedule 8812
   - Earned Income Credit → Schedule EIC
   - Education Credits → Form 8863

5. **Other Taxes:**
   - Self-employment tax → Schedule SE
   - Additional Medicare tax → Form 8959
   - Net investment income tax → Form 8960
